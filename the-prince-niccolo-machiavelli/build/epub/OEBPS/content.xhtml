Chapter I: How Many Kinds of Principalities There Are, and by What Means They Are Acquired

All states, all powers, that have held and hold rule over men have been and are either republics or principalities.

Principalities are either hereditary, in which the family has been long established; or they are new.

[Annotation: Machiavelli establishes the fundamental distinction between states founded by legitimacy and those acquired by force.]

The new are either entirely new, as was Milan to Francesco Sforza, or they are, as it were, members annexed to the hereditary state of the prince who has acquired them, as was the kingdom of Naples to that of the King of Spain.

Chapter II: Concerning Hereditary Principalities

I will leave out all discussion on republics, inasmuch as in another place I have written of them at length, and will address myself only to principalities.

[Annotation: Hereditary States: Easier to maintain because the subjects are accustomed to the family's rule, provided custom is respected.]

I say at once there are fewer difficulties in holding hereditary states, and those long accustomed to the family of their prince, than new ones...
