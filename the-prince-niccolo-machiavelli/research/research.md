## Research Notes

### Author Biography
Niccolò Machiavelli (1469–1527) was an Italian diplomat and political theorist. He served the Florentine Republic as a defense secretary and envoy, experiencing imprisonment and exile before writing The Prince, his most famous political treatise.

### Historical Context
Written in 1513, The Prince reflects Renaissance political thought and realpolitik strategies. The work remains influential in political science and leadership studies.

### Major Themes
- Power acquisition and retention
- Moral vs. immoral leadership
- Role of the military
- Statecraft and governance

### Timeline
- 1469: Birth of Machiavelli
- 1494: Florentine Republic establishes
- 1513: Machiavelli writes The Prince
- 1513–1518: Imprisonment and exile
- 1527: Death of Machiavelli
